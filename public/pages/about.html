Who I Am

I'm Alex Hugli, an expert contributor at Snorkel AI and backend software engineer. I benchmark and evaluate language models with rigorous end-to-end tests — writing the tests, running them, writing the solutions to compare against, and contributing to research. With 7+ years of programming experience, I attended the University of Washington studying Computer Science.

I specialize in Python, Java, C, and backend systems development. My work spans reinforcement learning agents, neural network architectures, LLM integration, and AI evaluation. I'm deeply passionate about open source software and believe in the transformative potential of artificial intelligence.

I started coding at a young age, driven by curiosity about how software systems work under the hood. That early passion led me through years of self-directed learning before I pursued formal education in computer science.

During my internship at Decent Apps Europe, I gained hands-on experience with decentralized systems, cloud infrastructure, and Linux administration. That experience reinforced my interest in building reliable, scalable software.

Outside of engineering, I enjoy running, meditation, and exploring new ideas at the intersection of technology and creativity.
